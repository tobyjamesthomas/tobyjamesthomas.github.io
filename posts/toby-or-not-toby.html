Toby or Not Toby

Updates on my life, health and where I'm headed next

May 1, 2019 - 1 minute read -
blog health

I have had six surgeries in my life. Two of which were when I was a baby, and the other four happened in the last year. My body is now riddled with battle scars, which I am fondly proud of, and I am on a new path of better health, hopefully for the rest of my life.

If you have been following my story thus far, youâ€™ll know that early last year, I had my colon removed due to a fierce battle with Ulcerative Colitis. Afterwards, I underwent three more surgeries to construct a j-pouch. My last surgery was at the end of January this year and my recovery has been going better than I could have ever hoped for.

It is truly unfathomable that I am alive and well today, living comfortably without my colon or an ostomy. I have regained the liberty to move, eat, sleep, run, jump and more without the anxiety of my ostomy and traumas of my past, and can use the washroom normally. These are things that I will never take for granted, and every day I am reminded by these simple actions that I am blessed. I am where I am today thanks to the many people in my life, my family, friends and doctors.
